Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly Election is conducted once every five years to elect representatives from constituencies across the state. The elected members form the state government and make important decisions related to administration and development.

The Election Commission of India manages and supervises the complete election process. Voting booths are arranged in cities, towns, and villages to help citizens vote safely and peacefully.

During the 2026 election, voter awareness programs were conducted throughout Tamil Nadu. Educational institutions, social organizations, and media channels encouraged people to participate actively in the democratic process.

Young voters played a major role in this election. Many first-time voters expressed interest in employment opportunities, digital development, environmental protection, and economic growth.

Election campaigns focused on development projects, public welfare, education support, healthcare improvements, infrastructure expansion, and women empowerment programs.

Tamil Nadu elections always attract huge public attention because of the strong political history of the state and the active participation of citizens in democratic activities.
